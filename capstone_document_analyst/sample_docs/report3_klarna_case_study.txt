Title: Klarna AI Customer Service Agent — Enterprise Case Study

Summary: Klarna, a fintech "buy now pay later" company, deployed an AI
customer service agent built on top of a large language model. By Q3
2025, the agent was handling roughly two-thirds of all customer service
chats, equivalent to the workload of 853 full-time employees.

Performance metrics: Average response time dropped from 11 minutes to
under 2 minutes, an 82% improvement. Klarna reported approximately $60
million in savings attributable to this deployment.

Risks identified: Industry analysis of similar large-scale agentic AI
deployments highlights several recurring risks: data leaks, unauthorized
changes to core systems, low visibility into the agent's individual
actions, and runaway costs from parallel or recursive tool calls. A
broader industry statistic notes that 88% of AI agent projects never
reach production, with the leading causes being infrastructure gaps
(41%), governance and security barriers (38%), and difficulty measuring
ROI (33%).

Lesson: The highest-ROI agentic AI deployments share three
characteristics: the agent owns a complete decision end-to-end, it runs
in production rather than as a pilot, and the system architecture is
matched to the actual risk level of the task being automated.
