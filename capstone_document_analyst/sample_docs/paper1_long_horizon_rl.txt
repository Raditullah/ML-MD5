Title: Reinforcement Learning for Long-Horizon Interactive LLM Agents

Abstract: Training LLM agents for long-horizon interactive tasks typically
requires expensive ground-truth action sequences or very large training
datasets. This paper introduces LOOP, a method that combines Proximal
Policy Optimization (PPO) with a leave-one-out baseline estimate and
per-token clipping, enabling effective reinforcement learning for
multi-turn LLM agents without those expensive requirements.

Key contribution: LOOP demonstrates that reinforcement learning can
effectively train interactive agents to perform long sequences of actions
(browsing, tool calls, multi-step reasoning) purely from environment
feedback, rather than imitation of pre-collected expert trajectories.

Method details: The leave-one-out baseline is computed by averaging the
returns of other samples within the same batch, removing the need for a
separately trained value network. Per-token clipping applies the PPO
clipped surrogate objective at the granularity of individual output
tokens rather than whole episodes, improving training stability for
long-generation agents.

Results: Agents trained with LOOP outperform baselines on multi-step web
navigation and tool-use benchmarks, achieving higher task completion
rates with significantly less training data than imitation-learning
approaches.
